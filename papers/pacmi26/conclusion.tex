\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

Systems incorporating language model components must decide how much resources to spend
on verification and where to spend it. 
We introduce the notion of epistemic observability to provide the cost surface
for making that decision.
We present different tiers of epsitemic observability for providing
different levels of verification, and build a tensor interface
at the logit-based observability tier to show how it can reduce the
budget required for reducing hallucinations and improving
accuracy.
